If your Lenovo ThinkPad (X390 Yoga – type 20NQ) powers ON only when the charger is plugged in, but the power button does NOT work on battery, then the problem is almost always one of these:

✅ 1. Battery is not detected or not connected
#

Go to BIOS and check:

Steps:

  1. Turn on laptop while holding F1 to enter BIOS.

  2. Go to Power or Battery section.

  3. Check if the battery shows:

    • Detected / Installed
    • Condition OK

If BIOS says No Battery Detected, then:

  • Battery connector might be loose
  • Battery is dead
  • Motherboard battery circuit is faulty

✅ 2. Battery is completely dead (0 V)
#

If the internal battery went to 0 volts, the laptop can only start with the charger plugged.

Try this:
#

  1. Plug the charger
  2. Hold the power button for 60 seconds
  3. Wait 10 minutes
  4. Try starting again on battery only

If still dead → battery is gone.

✅ 3. Internal battery disabled in BIOS
#

ThinkPads have a setting:

BIOS → Config → Power → Disable Built-in Battery

If this is enabled accidentally, the laptop will NOT power on without the charger.

Fix:

  1. Enter BIOS (F1)
  2. Go to Config → Power
  3. Make sure “Disable Built-in Battery” is NOT enabled

✅ 4. Power button PCB issue
#

ThinkPads use a small cable from the keyboard OR a small daughterboard for the power button.

If the signal is too weak on battery but works with charger → faulty power button board.

Solution:

  • Reseat cable
  • Replace the power button module (cheap part)

✅ 5. Motherboard Power Rail Failure
#

If the 3.3V/5V standby rail is weak, the laptop cannot wake from battery but can wake when charger boosts voltage.

Symptoms:

  • Power button does nothing on battery
  • But laptop turns on immediately when charger is connected

This requires repair (board-level).

🔧 Quick diagnostic steps for you
#

Try these one by one:

A. Reset the laptop
#

  1. Unplug the charger
  2. Press and hold the small reset pin on the bottom (Emergency Reset Hole)
  3. Plug charger
  4. Try again

B. Check if battery charges
#

While plugged in, in Windows/Linux:

cat /sys/class/power_supply/BAT0/status
cat /sys/class/power_supply/BAT0/capacity

If battery stays at 0% → battery dead or not detected.

C. Remove the bottom cover and reseat battery connector
#

The X390 Yoga has an internal battery. Reseat it:

  • Remove 7 screws
  • Lift cover
  • Disconnect and reconnect battery cable

From deep seek
#

Of course. This is a very specific and common symptom that points directly to a power delivery issue. The fact that the power button only works when the AC adapter is plugged in means your Lenovo ThinkPad is not recognizing or using its internal battery.

Here’s a breakdown of what’s happening and a step-by-step guide to diagnose and fix the problem.

The Core Problem
#

Your laptop is behaving as if there is no battery installed. When the power plug is connected, it can draw enough energy to start. When unplugged, it has no power source, so the button does nothing.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting Guide
#

Start from the top and work your way down.

Step 1: The Basic Power Reset (This fixes many issues)
#

This clears any temporary electrical glitches.

  1. Shut down the laptop completely.
  2. Unplug the AC power adapter from the laptop.
  3. Disconnect the internal battery. For your specific model (20NQ-S6M800), this likely requires removing the bottom case. You’ll need a small Phillips screwdriver.
    • Remove the screws and carefully pry off the bottom panel.
    • Locate the battery connector on the motherboard and unplug it.
  4. With both the AC adapter and battery disconnected, press and hold the power button for 20-30 seconds. This drains any residual power (flea current) from the capacitors.
  5. Reconnect the internal battery firmly.
  6. Put the bottom case back on.
  7. Plug in the AC adapter.
  8. Try to turn on the laptop with the power button.

Did this fix it? If not, proceed to the next step.

Step 2: Check Battery Status in Windows
#

If you can boot up with the power adapter plugged in, check what the operating system sees.

  1. Click the Battery Icon in your system tray (bottom-right corner of Windows).
  2. Does it show a battery percentage, or does it say “0% available,” “No battery detected,” or “Plugged in, not charging”?
  3. Go to Start Menu > Settings > System > Power & Battery > Battery.
  4. See if it provides any details about the battery’s health or status.

This will tell you if the problem is hardware-related (the OS sees nothing) or potentially a firmware/battery communication issue.

Step 3: Update Critical Software and Firmware
#

An outdated BIOS or driver can cause the motherboard to miscommunicate with the battery.

  1. Update BIOS/UEFI:

    • Open a web browser and go to the official Lenovo Support Website.
    • Enter your serial number (R9-QY7PQJ) or model number (20NQ-S6M800). The site will automatically find your product.
    • Go to the “Drivers & Software” section.
    • Look for a “BIOS/UEFI” update. Download and install the latest version. Ensure your laptop is plugged in during this entire process and do not interrupt it.

  2. Update Power Management Driver:

    • On the same Lenovo support page, look under the “Drivers” category for “Chipset” or “Power Management” drivers. Install any available updates.

Step 4: Check the Physical Hardware
#

If the software steps don’t work, the issue is likely physical.

  1. Inspect the AC Adapter: While the laptop works when plugged in, a faulty adapter can sometimes cause strange power issues. If you have access to another compatible Lenovo 65W or 45W adapter, test with it.
  2. Inspect the Battery Connection: When you opened the laptop for the power reset, did you make sure the battery connector was seated firmly and correctly on the motherboard? Unplug it and plug it back in to be sure.
  3. Inspect for Physical Damage: Look at the battery itself for any signs of swelling, leaking, or damage. If the battery is swollen, stop using the laptop immediately and do not charge it. A swollen battery is a fire hazard and needs to be replaced.

Most Likely Causes (Ranked)
#

  1. Failed Battery: This is the most common cause. Batteries have a limited lifespan (typically 2-4 years) and eventually lose the ability to hold a charge. If your laptop is more than a few years old, this is the prime suspect.
  2. Loose Battery Connection: The connector might have been jostled loose.
  3. Motherboard Issue (Power Circuitry): This is the worst-case scenario. A component on the motherboard responsible for managing power from the battery (like the charging IC) has failed. This is a complex and expensive repair.

Summary and Next Steps
#

  • First, perform the Power Reset (Step 1). It’s free and often works.
  • If that fails, your battery is most likely dead and needs to be replaced. You can search for a replacement using your model number 20NQ-S6M800 or the specific FRU (Field Replaceable Unit) number printed on the old battery.
  • If a new battery doesn’t solve the problem, then the issue is almost certainly with the motherboard, and you should contact a professional repair service like Lenovo’s own service center or a reputable third-party repair shop for a diagnosis.

Given the specific symptom, you are almost certainly looking at a battery replacement.
