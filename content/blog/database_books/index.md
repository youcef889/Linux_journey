Of course! 📚 Here’s a list of **excellent books** for learning **Databases** and especially **Relational Algebra**:

---

### Beginner-Friendly:

1. **"Database System Concepts"**  
   — *by Abraham Silberschatz, Henry Korth, S. Sudarshan*  
   ➔ A classic. Very clear explanations of relational algebra, relational calculus, and SQL.  
   ➔ Covers **theory** + **practical examples**.

2. **"Fundamentals of Database Systems"**  
   — *by Ramez Elmasri and Shamkant Navathe*  
   ➔ Very detailed. Good for understanding how relational algebra connects to real database systems.

3. **"Learning SQL"**  
   — *by Alan Beaulieu*  
   ➔ If you want to quickly become comfortable with **querying databases** (SQL), this book is simple and practical.  
   ➔ Not deep into theory, but helps you apply relational thinking.

---

### For deeper **Theory**:

4. **"Principles of Database and Knowledge-Base Systems"**  
   — *by Jeffrey D. Ullman*  
   ➔ Heavy on **relational algebra**, **relational calculus**, and **logic foundations**.  
   ➔ Best if you like a more mathematical approach.

5. **"Foundations of Databases"**  
   — *by Serge Abiteboul, Richard Hull, Victor Vianu*  
   ➔ Advanced and theoretical.  
   ➔ Covers relational algebra, logic programming, complexity of queries, etc.

---

### Bonus (free online resource):
- **"Database Management Systems"** — *by Raghu Ramakrishnan and Johannes Gehrke*  
  ➔ Some versions and lecture notes are available **online**.  
  ➔ Well-explained relational algebra with many examples.

---

### If you want a short plan:

- First  **Silberschatz**  ➔ easy intro + relational algebra basics.  
- Then  **Elmasri/Navathe**  ➔ details, SQL + design + normalization.  
- Then  **Ullman**  ➔ if you want deeper mathematical knowledge.
