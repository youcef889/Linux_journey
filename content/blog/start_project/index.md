---

**First: The Minimum Knowledge of Basic Management**

It is essential to have a general background on the fundamentals of management, such as:
- What is meant by financial management?
- How do you manage marketing?
- What are human resources?
- How do you manage operations, sales, and customer service?

You will need these basics from the very first day of the project, and you should continue to develop yourself and learn more over time. Most projects are similar in these management aspects.

---

**Second: Knowledge and Technical Skills Specific to the Field**

One of the most common mistakes some people make is entering a field they do not know the technical details of. For example:
- Managing a restaurant is different from managing a clothing store.
- Managing a medical clinic is not the same as managing an advertising agency.

If you do not have sufficient experience in the field, here are some available solutions for you:
1. Work in the field for a while to learn.
2. Partner with someone who has experience.
3. Hire someone with expertise.
4. Study the field seriously and learn from experience.

It is best to combine more than one method.

---

**Third: Sufficient Capital**

We will discuss this topic in more detail later, but you should know from now that:
Every project requires accurate calculations of establishment and operating costs.
There must be a financial reserve, as you may not make profits in the first year.
Don’t forget to allocate a budget for marketing; it is not a secondary matter but a fundamental factor in the success of the project.

---

**Fourth: Sufficient Time**

No projects are managed alone or operate without human effort, especially in their early stages. Managing a private project requires more time and effort than a job, and it may affect your personal life. Be prepared for this challenge.

---

**Fifth: An Entrepreneurial Mindset and Overcoming Myths**

You need to recognize the following facts:
- There are no guaranteed profits from the first month.
- No project is without risks.
- Freelancing is harder at the beginning than a job.
- The market does not forgive those who underestimate it or enter unprepared.

Learning should precede application. Do not enter the market to learn randomly… rather, learn first, then apply what you have learned.

---

**Sixth: Personal and Ethical Qualities**

These qualities make a real difference, even though many do not give them their due:
- Love for people and human interaction.
- Control over emotions.
- Ethics, honesty, and integrity.
- Determination and perseverance, as failure is a natural part of every beginning.
- Willingness to take calculated risks, as the absence of risks does not exist in the business world.

---

**In Summary:**

The first stage is the stage of preparation. If you are not well-prepared, it is better not to start the project at all. In the upcoming articles, God willing, we will discuss the second and third stages in more detail.
