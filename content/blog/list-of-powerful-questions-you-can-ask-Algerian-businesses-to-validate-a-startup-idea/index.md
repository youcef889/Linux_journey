Here’s a **list of powerful questions you can ask Algerian businesses** to validate a startup idea. The key is to **understand their problems, pain points, and willingness to pay**, without pushing your solution too early.

---

## 1️⃣ Start With General Business Workflow

* Can you describe a typical day in your business operations?
* What tasks take the most time for you or your team?
* Which tasks are repetitive or boring?
* Are there tasks that often cause mistakes or delays?
* How do you currently manage documents, reports, and approvals?

---

## 2️⃣ Pain Points & Challenges

* What is the most frustrating part of your workflow?
* Which processes are slow or inefficient?
* Have you ever lost important documents or data?
* Are there tasks that cost your company money or time?
* What do your employees complain about most regarding their tools or processes?

---

## 3️⃣ Current Solutions

* How do you currently solve [specific problem]?
* What software or tools do you use?
* Do these tools meet your needs? If not, what’s missing?
* Have you ever tried to improve or automate this process?
* How much time or money do you spend on this process each month?

---

## 4️⃣ Decision-Making & Priorities

* If you could automate or improve one process immediately, which would it be?
* How important is solving this problem for your business? (scale 1–10)
* What would happen if this problem is not solved?

---

## 5️⃣ Willingness to Pay

* Would you pay for a tool that solves this problem?
* How much would you expect to pay per month/year for a solution?
* What features would make a tool worth paying for?
* Would you prefer a local or international solution? Why?

---

## 6️⃣ Feedback on Early Concept (Optional)

* If we built a tool that could [solve problem X], would you use it?
* What features would make this tool most useful for your business?
* Would you like to be part of early testing?

---

### ⚠️ Interview Tips

1. **Don’t pitch** your solution first. Focus on the problem.
2. **Listen more than you speak**. Take notes.
3. **Ask follow-up questions** like “why is that a problem?” or “how often does that happen?”
4. Aim for **10–30 interviews** for validation.
5. Look for **patterns**, not single opinions.
