Validating a startup idea **before building it** is one of the most important steps. Many startups fail because they **build first and ask customers later**. The goal of validation is to prove that **people actually need and will pay for your solution**. 🚀

---

# 1️⃣ Clearly Define the Problem

Start with the **problem**, not the product.

Ask yourself:

* Who has this problem?
* How often does it happen?
* How painful is it?
* How are people solving it today?

Example:

```text
Problem: Companies lose time searching for documents.
Current solution: Paper folders and email attachments.
```

If the problem is **frequent and expensive**, it's a good candidate.

---

# 2️⃣ Identify the Exact Customer

A startup fails when it tries to serve **everyone**.

Define your target:

Example:

```text
Target customer:
Small construction companies
10–50 employees
Operating in Algeria
```

The more specific the target, the better.

---

# 3️⃣ Talk to Real People

This is the **most important step**.

Interview at least:

```
10–30 potential customers
```

Ask questions like:

* What is the hardest part of your workflow?
* What tasks waste your time?
* What tools do you currently use?
* What do you hate about those tools?

⚠️ Do **not ask if they like your idea**.
People often say yes but never pay.

Instead ask about **their real problems**.

---

# 4️⃣ Check If They Already Pay for Solutions

A good sign is when people already spend money.

Example:

```
Company paying accountant $300/month
because accounting software is missing.
```

This means the **problem is expensive**.

---

# 5️⃣ Look at Competitors

Competition is **a good sign**, not a bad one.

If competitors exist, it means:

```
Market demand exists
```

Look at companies like:

* Shopify
* Odoo

Then ask:

* What do customers complain about?
* What features are missing?

Your opportunity is **improving something existing**.

---

# 6️⃣ Test With a Simple Landing Page

Before building the software, create a **simple website explaining the idea**.

Example content:

```
Headline
Problem description
Your solution
Sign-up form
```

Tools people often use:

* Carrd
* Webflow

Goal:

```
Collect emails of interested users
```

---

# 7️⃣ Try to Get Pre-Orders

The strongest validation is **someone paying before the product exists**.

Example:

```
"Early access price: $10/month"
```

Even **5 paying customers** is strong validation.

---

# 8️⃣ Build a Small Prototype (MVP)

Do not build the full product.

Instead build:

```
Minimum Viable Product
```

Only the **core feature** that solves the problem.

Example for document management:

MVP features:

* upload document
* search document
* basic folders

Everything else can come later.

---

# 9️⃣ Measure Real Usage

Important metrics:

* signups
* daily usage
* retention
* paid users

If people **sign up but never use it**, the idea may not work.

---

# 🔟 Decide to Continue or Pivot

After testing:

If customers say:

```
"This saves me time"
```

→ continue building.

If they say:

```
"I don't really need this"
```

→ change the idea.

---

# Simple Startup Validation Flow

```
Idea
 ↓
Problem interviews
 ↓
Landing page
 ↓
Preorders
 ↓
MVP
 ↓
First paying customers
```

---

# A Powerful Rule (Startup Secret)

Good startup ideas usually have this pattern:

```
People already trying to solve the problem
but the current solutions are terrible.
```

Your job is to **make the solution easier**.
